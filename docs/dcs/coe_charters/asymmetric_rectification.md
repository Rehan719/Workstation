# Asymmetric-Drive Rectification CoE Charter

**Mission**: Development and deployment of asymmetric-drive learning systems that convert transient fluctuations into durable structural memory, inspired by cellular specialization mechanisms.

**Key Responsibilities**:
- Implementation of topological ratchets for architectural stability.
- Monitoring environmental fluctuations and identifying rectification candidates.
- Managing residual cost accounting for reorganization events.

**Core Agents**:
- `RectificationLearningAgent`: Identifies phase-aligned fluctuations for rectification.
- `TopologicalRatchetAgent`: Enforces structural persistence post-rectification.
- `ResidualCostAuditor`: Tracks and reports the information-processing ledger.

**OKR Examples**:
- Objective: Convert 90% of identified high-impact fluctuations into structural memory.
- Objective: Maintain residual cost of reorganization below 5% of total system entropy.

**Inter-CoE Dependencies**:
- Policy & Governance (for approval of major structural ratchets).
- Advanced Cognitive Research (for algorithm refinement).
